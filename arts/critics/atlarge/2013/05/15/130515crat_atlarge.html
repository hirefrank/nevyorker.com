A Critic at Large

Handmade Communication

How one startup chats without wires.

by Jeeves Schiedner May 15, 2013

On a calm Spring afternoon, Sorry! (pronounced SORE-E in a maple leaf accent) is shouted from across the room. Others echo and repeat leading to Apologies in a round reverberating through the office.

Christopher Stumm, a devout Canadian, sitting in the corner with headphones on and dog-in-lap pauses and swivels in his chair grinning ear to ear. That's his calling.

His misspellings, mispronunciations and unassuming demeanor endear him to his colleagues. When he isn't hugging someone he is listening and quickly becoming the equivalent of the Moscow-Washington hotline for internal communications at Etsy, an online marketplace projecting $1 billion in sales this year. That's a lot of loonies.

Of course it wasn't always that way. Stumm joined Etsy in the bustling Summer of 2011. During this period, Etsy perferred emails over in-person discussions and quick IMs over handshakes and winks. Following Stumm's arrival everything started to change. Most notably, the company set the Peer-to-Peer DIY E-Commerce Marketplace world on fire with key changes in search and advertising products later that Fall.

Shown in his formative years in Switzerland, Christopher Stumm built a sophisticated communications network by dogsitting and coffee fetchingall the while playing the role of lovable goofball.

"It's hard to say it was a coincidence with any amount of confidence," says Dan McKinley, Principal Engineer, on Stumm's involvment.

Etsy's version of face-time has continued to evolve in the last few years with Stumm as the invisible ringleader. How does he do it? He has regular coffee check-ins (he always orders an espresso) during the week with key representatives throughout different departments.

Oh and if that wasn't enough, he routinely dogsits — which next to food — is the closet way to an Etsian's heart. His propsentiy to always have a dog in his lap, has led to the fan-site, stummsitting.com, which boasts "Need a dog sitter? He has a lap" as the title.
